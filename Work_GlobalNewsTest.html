Infill Residential Tower Coming to Chicago's Lakefront

A rendering of the proposal at Lake Shore Drive and Waveland Avenue. Courtesy of Chicago's 46th Ward
29 August 2019 | Chicago, United States

City Club Apartments has announced plans to develop a rare infill site on Chicago's famed Lake Shore Drive.

The proposal, presented to constituents of Chicago's 46th Ward, includes two mixed-use towers atop a sitewide base. The northern tower stands at 206 feet and 19 stories and its counterpart to the south stands at 66 feet and five stories in its current iteration. The number of residential units is yet to be determined as the developer continues to solicit feedback from City officials and the public.

Directly to the west of the site, the New York Private Residences towers 48 stories above the Lakeview neighborhood and currently enjoys unobstructed views of Lake Michigan. CCA will have to will have to contend with its nextdoor neighbors to build their towers as proposed. Original plans for the NYPR included a second tower on the site of the proposed building, and units in the existing building face Lake Michigan at 45-degree angles. A site plan also shows a 24-foot access driveway connecting the two developments via Inner Lake Shore Drive and Waveland Avenue.
